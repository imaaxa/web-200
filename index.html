Patricia Gilliam is the author of over ten sci-fi and superhero books. Some of these books include the Hannaria Series, Thaw in Hugh Howey's: The Silo Series, stories in multiple anthologies, the current Heroes of Corvus Series, and the soon to be released Horizons Beyond. After writing her first book in 2009, she has never lost her desire to continue writing. Writing for all these years has always overshadowed her bachelor’s degrees in psychology and mass communications from East Tennessee State University.

Patricia grew up in Greenville, Kingsport, and Blountville, Tennessee. After graduating college in 2005 with a BA in Psychology and a BA in Mass Communication, she moved to Knoxville, Tennessee. Once there, she set out to write and publish her first book, Out of the Gray, in 2009. Patricia has made appearances as a writer at local and regional sci-fi conferences. She been sought out for interviews and has been dubbed "Con Queen" by Lauren Moore at Keystroke Medium.

Patricia can almost always be found working on her next novel or helping aspiring writers through workshops or answering questions online. When Patricia is not writing, her and her husband since May of 2005, Cory Gilliam, travel to attend different sci-fi and writers’ conventions all over the south east.

Three Act Structure

Whenever Patricia goes out to teach inspiring writers, she uses what she has learned from other people on how to layout stories. Most novels, movies, and television shows use a set of markers or beats to help structure how the tale unfolds. These beats here, commonly known as the Hero's Journey, are derived from the Save the Cat Method. They were greatly inspired by the book The Man of a Thousand Faces.

  • Act 1

    A before snapshot of your hero, and their world.
    An exploration of the hero's status quo life, and all its flaws, where we learn what the hero's life looks like before its epic transformation.
    A statement made by a character, typically not the hero, that hints at what the hero's arc will be, what the hero must learn or discover before the end of the book.
    An inciting incident of life-changing event that happens to the hero, which will catapult them into a new world or new way of thinking.
    A rection sequence where the hero debates what they will do next.

  • Act 2

    The moment the hero decides to accept the call to action, leave their comfort zone, try something new, or venture into a new world or a new way of thinking.
    The introduction of a new character of characters who will ultimately serve to help the hero learn the theme.
    This is where we see the hero in their new world. They are either loving it, or hating it, succeeding, or floundering.
    Literally the middle of the story, where the fun and games culminates in either a false victory or a false defeat.
    Depending upon the outcome of the midpoint, this will be an upward or downward path for the hero. Upward after a false defeat or downward after a false victory.
    The lowest point of the novel, an action beat where something happens to the hero, combined with the internal bad guys, pushes the hero to rock bottom.
    Here the hero takes time to process everything that has happened thus far. At this point, the hero should be worse off than they were at the beginning of the novel.

  • Act 3

    The point where the hero realizes what they must do to not only fix all of their problems created in act two, but more important, fix themself.
    Bad guys are destroyed, flaws are conquered, lovers are reunited. Not only is the hero's world saved, but it's a better place than it was before..
    A mirror beat to the opening image. This is an after snapshot that reveals who the hero is, after going through the epic and satisfying transformation..
